Sensing Device

Why

It all started with a question: how can a device be designed to enhance someone's sensing ability?

What

A wearable device that enhances the user's sense of hearing by giving them auditory feedback, capturing the sound of winds and other movement.

How

Cardboard, wooden dowels, string.

A device that enhances your sensing ability

It is recommended for the wearer to close their eyes and fully immerse themselves in the directions of the sounds.

Also enables users to hear their own movement.

Collapsible for easy transport

Natural Process Machine

Why

I set out to gain a better understanding of the natural process of pollination, specifically within bees. The goal was to create a fictional machine that embodies that process.

What

A fictional machine that embodies the internal flow and processes of pollination, with further consideration for the people and context around it.

How

SolidWorks, laser-cut acrylic, 3D printed PLA.

Outer tanks mimic the repetitive regurgitation phenomenon in the "honey stomach" of bees

Left: arrow drawing of forces in play when a bee collects pollen from a flower. Right: environmental and human context of the machine.

Plaster Casting

Why

I was intrigued by the process of translating a 2D pattern into 3D forms, and and how these forms would interact with light through repetition.

What

Seashell inspired 3D pattern that is visually dynamic when interacting with light.

How

I carved foam to make a silicone mold, which was used to cast the individual plaster pieces. They were then sanded to a smooth finish.
